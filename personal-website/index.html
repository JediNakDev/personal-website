Energy and exergy analysis

with cost-efficiency comparison of modified phase change material (PCM) integrated with solar panel thermal system

  • -> Oral presented at the 18th Siam Physics Congress (SPC2023)
  • -> Accepted for publication in the Journal of Physics: Conference Series (JPCS).

This study explores the use of Phase Change Materials (PCM) in solar panels to improve their efficiency and cost-effectiveness. By integrating PCMs, temperature fluctuations are minimized, optimizing power generation. The research involves modeling, simulations, and energy analysis of various PCMs like Paraffin wax and Sodium acetate trihydrate. Temperature and light intensity data are collected in Rayong province, and numerical methods are used to determine temperature values of each layer. The study shows that PCM-integrated solar panels can generate up to 11.1% more power with increased cost-efficiency.
